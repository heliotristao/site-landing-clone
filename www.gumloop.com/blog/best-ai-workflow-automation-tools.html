10 best AI workflow automation tools I'm using in 2026

Omid Ghiam
September 17, 2025
•
28 min read

On a fall afternoon in 2022, I opened my laptop in the New York City public library (on 5th Avenue and 42nd Street) and started playing around with what at the time was called the OpenAI playground.

It was an interface where you got to talk to a chatbot and it said stuff back (like Clever Bot, anyone remember that?)

And it blew my mind. 

A couple months later, ChatGPT was officially launched and it changed the AI game forever.

As the year went on, more and more tools started to pop up. And the use cases for AI started to grow.

Now, AI could actually interact with our everyday tools. And this resulted in the birth of AI workflow automation tools.

As someone who's an AI and marketing enthusiast, I've been using these tools pretty extensively for some time now. And in the past couple of years, some amazing platforms have come out.

But also, some pretty shady tools have also launched. And at times, it can be hard to know which platforms are smoke and mirrors and which ones actually create a notable step function in our productivity.

So in this article, I want to go over the best AI workflow tools that I have tested and know to be worth checking out. I'll also go over some examples of what you can do with them.

Let's get started.

What is an AI automation workflow tool?

An AI automation workflow tool is a platform that allows you to connect your everyday tools with an LLM to give them AI functionality. With these tools, you can streamline entire workflows that you currently do manually.

You can connect your favorite apps like Gmail, Slack, Google Sheets, Notion, or whatever tools you use daily, and then add AI reasoning on top of them. Instead of just moving data from one app to another (like traditional automation), these tools let you actually process, analyze, and make decisions with that data using AI.

For example, you could build a workflow that monitors your company's mentions on social media, uses AI to analyze the sentiment of each mention, and then automatically creates tasks in your project management tool based on whether the feedback is positive or needs immediate attention.

The cool part is that you don't need to know how to code or hire a developer. These platforms give you drag-and-drop interfaces where you can visually connect your apps and tell the AI what you want it to do in natural language.

What are examples of workflow automation?

AI workflow automation can handle pretty much any repetitive task you can think of. The possibilities are honestly endless, but here are some practical examples that people are building right now:

Content and marketing workflows:

  • YouTube to SEO blog agent that takes your video content and automatically turns it into optimized blog posts
  • Newsletter creator agent that pulls content from multiple sources and creates your weekly newsletter
  • Social media sentiment agent that monitors brand mentions and categorizes feedback

Sales and lead generation:

  • Scrape and categorize leads agent that finds potential customers and organizes them by quality
  • Personalized outreach agent that researches prospects and writes custom emails
  • Brand listening agent that tracks competitors and industry trends

Productivity and admin:

  • AI Slackbot agent that answers team questions using your company knowledge base
  • Inbox summarizer agent that gives you daily email digests with key action items
  • SEO analyzer agent that audits your content and suggests improvements

This is just the tip of the iceberg though. I wrote a whole article on more AI agent examples you can check out to see what's possible. It’s actually insane. There are even now agentic AI tools that make it easier than ever to create these workflows.

Now, before I get into my top AI workflow automation tools, let me briefly go over how AI can actually automate this stuff.

How can AI automate workflows?

AI can automate workflows by letting LLMs handle decision-making processes between your tools. What I mean by this is that instead of just moving data from Point A to Point B (like traditional automation tools), AI can actually think about that data and make smart decisions about what to do next.

This is so powerful because LLMs can process unstructured data (like emails, documents, or social media posts) and turn it into structured actions.

By now, we all know LLMs and generative AI can hallucinate pretty badly. And when it does, it makes many AI tools unreliable.

But when you give LLMs real data from your existing tools, it takes that data and works with it (so little to no hallucination). It doesn’t need to make up data because your tools are supplying it.

The LLMs can read, analyze, summarize, categorize, and even generate responses based on context. With some of the tools I’m about to show you, you can literally tell the AI what you want instead of learning complex programming logic.

Now that you understand what these tools can do, let's jump into the best AI workflow automation platforms you can use to build these smart automations for yourself or your team.

What is the difference between workflow automation and AI?

Workflow automation refers to building systems that automate repetitive tasks. The tools that help you do this have been around for a while, well before the whole AI craze started. AI (artificial intelligence), on the other hand, is the process of using machine learning to create systems and products that can help humans improve their productivity.

When you combine AI with workflow automation, you now get a system where you can connect your existing day-to-day tools like Gmail, Slack, Microsoft Teams/Power Automate, WordPress, and whatever tech tool you use, and you can combine it with the powers of LLMs (large language models).

With this, you get a new wave of tools like the ones I listed above that can help you take your automated workflows to an entirely new level. For example, now you can tell LLMs like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini to access your existing tools like a Google Sheet, and then tell them to write or read information within that spreadsheet. From there, you can create other automations that can send you a text or an email when something is updated in that spreadsheet, and all of this can happen automatically.

That's why tools like Gumloop, Zapier, and n8n are really awesome because you can stack different workflows together and use AI to interact with all of your existing tools. Essentially, now you have the ability to give your favorite tools the power of AI.
