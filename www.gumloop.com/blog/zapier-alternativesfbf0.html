Top 10 best Zapier alternatives I’ve tested in 2026

Omid Ghiam
September 23, 2025
•
18 min read

I’m a huge Zapier fan.

But over the past couple of years, with the rise of AI and LLMs, I’ve found myself constantly experimenting with new automation tools.

Especially with AI agents being all the craze now, I wanted to find a tool that had the automation features I love about Zapier with the AI capabilities of modern LLMs.

This way, I can not only create automated workflows between my apps, but I could use ChatGPT or Claude to “talk” to my apps and execute tasks on my behalf.

That’s when I started my search for Zapier alternatives that had an AI twist. I tried over a dozen different platforms, and today I’m going to give you a list of my favorite ones that I actually use.

For each tool, I’ll explain what it does, who it’s best for, and what I personally like (and don’t like) about it.

But first, let’s go over what to actually look for when finding an alternative to Zapier. I can tell you what is best, but I think it’s important for you to also understand what will truly fit your own needs based on the nuances of your business.

So first, let’s go over that.

What to look for in a Zapier alternative

I don’t know the exact reason why you might be switching away from Zapier. It could be the price, lack of AI features, or something else.

While I love the concept of “zaps” and different connectors with Zapier, I’ve found through my own experience that there are a lot of underrated tools that can do exactly what Zapier does.

Some of these tools are great for all use cases, while others are good at very specific ones like customer service, finance, marketing, operations, or something else.

So first, let’s make sure we know what to look for when selecting a tool from the list below. Here’s what you should consider when finding a Zapier alternative:

  • Can it integrate with your existing tools? Maybe you have a trusted CRM with tons of data already. Or you have a specific workflow using Semrush for your SEO. Whatever it is, make sure that the platform you choose can integrate with your existing tech stack.
  • Is it AI native? Today, everything is AI. So it’s a good idea to find an automation tool that is built with an AI-first approach. This means it should be able to integrate with all the modern LLMs so it can communicate with tools in your tech stack. It should also be able to integrate with MCP servers.
  • Is it fun to work with? Most of our time during weekdays is filled with work. So you might as well use tools that are fun to use. This means it has a user-friendly interface and the UI/UX just makes sense. I love well-designed tools, and I made sure to include Zapier alternatives below that fit my own criteria of that.
  • Does it give you a competitive edge? Of course it’s important for any tool to fulfll your automation needs. But you should also find tools that are constantly evolving to help your business make more money (or save more time). Find platforms that are constantly adding new features (and showing you how to use them).
  • Is there good support (and resource)? Automation tools aren’t the simplest of tools out there. So make sure whatever platform you choose has a community around it. Is the customer service good? Are there templates and YouTube videos that show you how to use the tool? While not the most important criteria for some, for me this matters a lot.

While there’s a bunch of nitty gritty stuff we can go over for evaluating these tools, I truly believe the ones I listed above give you a good north star for what to pick. And below, I listed out 10 of my favorite Zapier alternatives, all of which I mention who it’s best for.

Alright let’s get into it.

1. Gumloop

  • Best for: AI-powered workflow automation for non-technical teams
  • Pricing: Free, then starts at $37/month
  • What I like: Built-in AI assistant (Gummie), generous free plan with premium LLM access, and Interfaces feature to turn automations into apps

Gumloop is an AI automation platform that I've been using more and more recently. It's one of the closest alternatives to Zapier that I've found, except with an AI twist.

It's built for non-technical marketers, founders, HR managers, sales reps, and customer service teams. The tool essentially gives you the power of an AI engineer without having to know how to code.

All you do is build your automated workflows by dragging your favorite apps onto a visual canvas and connecting them together with conditional logic.

Just like Zapier, Gumloop lets you integrate all your favorite apps. And with guMCP, you can integrate with almost any application that has an MCP server. What makes Gumloop different from a platform like Zapier is that it takes it a step further by allowing you to add a layer of AI using LLMs like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, or whatever to integrate AI into your automations.

I personally use it to help me run my content marketing agency, but I know some huge brands also use it. Companies like Shopify, Instacart, and Webflow use Gumloop internally to automate processes across their hundreds of employees. So the platform has been created with enterprise customers in mind as well.

Some things I like about Gumloop:

  • Clean UI/UX that makes it easy to build any automated workflow
  • Has an AI assistant named Gummie that can help you build any automation and debug errors
  • Generous free plan and all paid plans include access to any premium LLM (with or without an API key)
  • Create AI-enhanced decision making where AI can route and choose the next best steps in your automations
  • Tons of templates so you can start building right away
  • Gumloop's Interfaces feature allows you to turn your automations into apps that your team members or friends can use

Here are some things that could be improved:

  • Still a fairly new platform and they're adding new features constantly, so the UI can change sometimes
  • Requires that you already have an existing workflow that you want to automate

Overall, it's an amazing platform and I've been using it for the past 9 months. I found myself slowly moving my Zapier workflows into Gumloop. So if you're a solo operator (like me) or someone who works in-house at an agency or startup, you need to check this out.

2. Make

  • Best for: Budget-friendly visual workflow automation with lots of app integrations
  • Pricing: Free, then starts at $10.59/month
  • What I like: Over 2,500 pre-built apps, MCP server support, and lots of educational resources on YouTube

Make (formerly Integromat) is a popular AI automation tool very similar to Zapier. It has the same visual interface where you can drag your apps into a workflow and connect them with conditional logic.

The platform has become popular among no-code enthusiasts and even AI service providers. It's fairly budget-friendly compared to other tools in this space, so a lot of small startups and freelancers on a budget like to use the platform.

Whether you're building your own automations for your business or helping other businesses build out their automations, Make is a pretty solid choice if you're already familiar with how Zapier works.

Some things I like about Make:

  • Popular platform with lots of educational resources on YouTube
  • Includes over 2,500 pre-built apps
  • Has an MCP server so you can connect to most apps
  • Allows you to connect to LLMs to build AI agents

Here are some things that could be improved:

  • The UI/UX can feel a bit outdated and clunky
  • Not as stable as a platform like Zapier

Overall, I would say Make is the budget-friendly version of Zapier. It's a bit more downgraded and not as polished, but it does help you build simple automations with the same power that Zapier would (at a fraction of the cost).

3. n8n

  • Best for: Self-hosted workflow automation for developers and technical teams
  • Pricing: Starts at $24/month (hosted)
  • What I like: Self-hosting features, wide range of community templates, and used by big companies like Microsoft and Vodafone

n8n is a workflow automation platform that has been growing in popularity. It's similar to Make, however it does have more advanced features. The platform is known for having self-hosting capabilities, making it a popular choice for developers.

It does lean a bit more on the technical side, so if you're not used to using low-code tools, there might be a steep learning curve.

Similar to Gumloop, the platform can help you automate literally anything, from IT operations, DevOps, sales, marketing, customer service, and a ton more. It's like a catch-all tool for any use case that requires you to use AI to automate repetitive tasks.

My only gripe with the tool is that you do need to use your own API key if you want to use LLMs like ChatGPT or Claude. Unlike Gumloop that gives you access to premium LLMs with just a single subscription, you will need a separate subscription for every LLM that you use.

Some things I like about n8n:

  • Super popular platform with tons of free resources on YouTube
  • Includes self-hosting options for developers
  • Has a wide range of templates created by community members
  • Used by some big companies like Microsoft, Vodafone, Wayfair, and more

Here are some things that could be improved:

  • It's more of a low-code platform, so you will need some technical knowledge
  • Similar to Make, the interface can feel a bit clunky and old
  • Requires you to have your own API key for LLMs

Overall, n8n is a solid choice if you're looking to replace Zapier. It's a great platform for creating automated workflows, however the only reason why I like Gumloop more is because it's a bit more user-friendly to build with.

5. IFTTT

  • Best for: Simple personal automations and basic work tasks
  • Pricing: Free, then starts at $3.99/month
  • What I like: Insanely affordable, simple to use with iOS and Android apps, and used by hundreds of thousands of people

IFTTT, which stands for "if this then that," is one of the original automation tools that ever existed alongside Zapier. It's the most basic tool on this list and it's mostly great for personal automations.

If you just want simple automations like getting the weather forecast every day at a certain time, or even if you have work-related things like setting up events in Google Calendar, then this is the platform for you.

It's also super affordable and it's the cheapest tool on this list. So if you just need simple automations for your personal life (and a little bit for your work life), I highly recommend checking out IFTTT.

Some things I like about IFTTT:

  • Insanely cheap and affordable
  • Simple to use and has iOS and Android apps
  • Used by hundreds of thousands of people, so it's quite reliable

Here are some things that could be improved:

  • Not the best for creating advanced workflows
  • Can feel a bit outdated
  • Doesn't integrate with every modern tool that you need

Overall, if complex functionality and automating business processes is not high on your list, and you really need just something to automate your personal life, this is the platform for you.

Is there anything better than Zapier?

Yes, I would say Gumloop is a better alternative if you’re looking for an AI-native platform (with a great free plan). And n8n is a great alternative to Zapier if you’re looking for something that is self-hosted and is focused more on business process automation.

However, Zapier is still incredibly popular in the no-code community for good reason. It's reliable and has tons of integrations. But if you want something that feels similar to Zapier while adding modern AI capabilities, Gumloop is the closest thing I’ve found.

After testing all these platforms over the past couple of years, the biggest thing I've learned is that the best automation tool is the one you'll actually use consistently. Each platform on this list has its strengths, whether that's Make's budget-friendly pricing, n8n's self-hosting capabilities, or IFTTT's simplicity.

But here's the reality is that you need workflows that can think through problems and make smart decisions based on context.

If you're ready to move beyond simple automations and want to build workflows that can actually reason through complex tasks using AI, I'd recommend starting with Gumloop. You can start building your first AI-powered automation right away, without the need to pull out your credit card or even make an account.

The combination of a clean interface, built-in AI capabilities, and a generous free plan makes it the easiest way to see what's possible when you combine automation with modern LLMs. Plus, with Gummie helping you build and troubleshoot, you're never stuck figuring things out alone.
